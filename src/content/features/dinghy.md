The dinghy gets used hard on a cruising boat, and this one is set up accordingly. A full-size Zodiac 12.5' with center console and wheel, electric start, electric trim, and a 25hp 4-stroke with fewer than 100 hours on it — gets on plane, hauls four adults, and covers real distance.

The hidden gem: the original owners specified an auxiliary aluminum gasoline tank built into the starboard sugar scoop with a filler hose. You pull the dinghy up to the stern, connect the hose, and refill from the boat. Top off the main tank once a season and you never think about dinghy gas again.

- Zodiac 12.5' YL380DL center console dinghy with new Hypalon tubes
- Suzuki 25hp 4-stroke outboard — electric start, electric trim, under 100 hours
- Onboard aluminum auxiliary gas tank in the starboard sugar scoop — with refill hose for the dinghy
- Tachometer / speedometer, running lights, bilge pump
- Teak aft platform with integral dinghy chocks
- Boom extension for dinghy lift halyard (acts as secondary main halyard)
