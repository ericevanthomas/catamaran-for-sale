question: In what condition are the sails?
answer: |-
  When we purchased the boat, this was another item we expected to replace right away because the seller did not know when the sails had last been replaced. However, when we did the sea trial, we found the sails to be in better condition than expected, and we opted to hold off on replacement. 

  I would categorize them as serviceable. The genoa still has quite good shape, and all sails are of  high-modulus cross-cut Dacron. The main's numerous full battens give it decent overall shape, but the leech is starting to look a little loose, and the UV covers on the genoa and storm jib need replacement. In my opinion, you can sail the islands for probably the next three years with these sails and do perfectly fine, but new canvas would likely add a half a knot of boat speed and be advisable if you are planning to cross any oceans.
