Installed new by us. A lot of Rainman installs are bucket-and-hose setups — great when you buy the unit, less great five years in when you're pickling it on deck in a crosswind. We had ours plumbed properly into the boat from day one.

You can fill either water tank individually with production water, and when the boat sits for a while you pickle the system by adding chemical to the unit's own reservoir — no buckets, no disassembly. It's the kind of thing you only appreciate the tenth time you use it.

- New 40 gallon-per-hour Rainman watermaker
- Plumbed directly into the boat's freshwater system
- Fill either tank individually with fresh production water
- Pickle-in-place via the unit's own reservoir — add chemical, run, done
- Supplements 211 gallons of tank capacity
- Runs on generator or inverter power from the 1,280Ah lithium bank
