Most of the catamarans we looked at put the cockpit right at water level or perched the helm way up on a flybridge — the first feels exposed, the second feels isolated. This design splits the difference. The cockpit is enclosed by proper combings and bolsters, wide side decks run all the way around, and the helm sits right there with everyone else, slightly elevated for sight lines but not up out of the conversation. Whoever's driving is still part of what's happening.

With kids on board, that mattered more than we expected.

- Deep combings and bolsters enclose the cockpit seating area
- Wide, walk-around side decks on both hulls
- Helm station within the cockpit — slightly elevated for visibility, but not separated
- Big sliding glass doors integrate the salon and cockpit into one space
- New outdoor cockpit cushions (Feb 2022)
- New weather shield between salon and bimini (2022)
- Bimini re-gel coated (2023)
- New hatch covers (2023)
- Ceramic film on salon windows and sliding door (2022)
- Shade film on helm sliding panel (Oct 2021)
- All cockpit locker latches replaced (Oct 2021)
