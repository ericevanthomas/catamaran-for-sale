A cruising catamaran really wants two headsails: a big reaching/downwind genoa for light air, and a small self-tacking jib for upwind work, heavy weather, or — practically speaking — when you want to slow the boat down at night and sleep better. Both are on furlers, so neither decision requires going forward on a pitching deck.

- 130% furling genoa — 8.3oz high-modulus cross-cut Dacron, foam luff, UV cover
- Small self-tacking furling jib (doubles as a storm jib) — 8.3oz with UV cover
- Both on Profurl furlers
- Two reefing lines and luff pendants
