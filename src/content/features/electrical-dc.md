The new 1,300Ah lithium house bank was installed professionally by marine electricians in St. Augustine, with additional safety cutoffs and kill switches added on top of the factory BMS — a belt-and-suspenders setup. Fully charged, the bank holds for days under normal cruising loads.

The solar panels are positioned well — on the coach roof and bimini — so they avoid most of the usual shading issues and routinely put out close to their rated output. The engines also carry secondary high-output alternators, so any time you're motoring you're charging the bank hard. Between solar, alternators, and a short generator run every few days to make water, we rarely think about power.

- LiFePO4 lithium house bank — 1,300Ah @ 12V (upgraded from 1,100Ah at purchase)
- Professionally installed by marine electricians in St. Augustine — extra safety cutoffs and kill switches beyond factory BMS
- 2× 400W (800W total) solar panels on coach roof / bimini — positioned for minimal shading
- EPEVER Tracer421SBN MPPT 40A solar regulator with MT50 remote meter (2022)
- 2× 80A Yanmar primary alternators + 2× 120A AMPLE secondary high-output alternators on engines
- Smart regulators on secondary alternators (rebuilt July 2023)
- 2× engine-start batteries (12V 65Ah)
- House bank / start batteries can be linked via crossover switch
- 3× battery isolator switches
- Mastervolt Masterview Easy MK11 battery management system
- 4× 200A BMS, programmable with Bluetooth (Oct 2021)
- 500A over-volt battery protection relay (Oct 2021)
- Mastershunt 500 12V shunt and Mass Combi 100A battery charger
- D400 12V wind generator with stop/run regulator and dump resistor
- Photocell auto-anchor-light (on/off control)
- 12V distribution panel, circuit breakers, and fuses
