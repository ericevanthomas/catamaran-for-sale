Installed new by us. The Rainman Modular AC-140 HighOutput is the top unit in their AC line — two 40″ Dow Filmtec RO membranes, a 316 stainless steel General Pump high-pressure pump, and a Jabsco lift pump. Production runs 26–37 gallons per hour depending on water temperature, and the whole system draws about 1,300 watts in steady state — easy work for the generator or the 3kW Victron inverter.

A lot of Rainman installs are bucket-and-hose setups — great when you buy the unit, less great five years in when you're pickling it on deck in a crosswind. We had ours plumbed properly into the boat from day one. You can fill either water tank individually with production water, and when the boat sits for a while you pickle the system by adding chemical to its own reservoir — no buckets, no disassembly.

- Rainman Modular AC-140 HighOutput — installed new
- Up to 37 GPH (26–37 depending on water temperature)
- Two 40" Dow Filmtec RO membranes
- 316 stainless steel General Pump WMR high-pressure pump
- Jabsco impeller lift pump with TEFC induction motor
- 115V or 230V capable — we run it from either voltage
- ~1,300W steady-state draw — runs comfortably off the generator or the 3kW Victron
- Plumbed directly into the boat's freshwater system
- Fill either tank individually with fresh production water
- Pickle-in-place via the unit's own reservoir — add chemical, run, done
- Five 5-micron pleated paper pre-filter cartridges included; non-proprietary consumables
- Supplements 211 gallons of tank capacity
