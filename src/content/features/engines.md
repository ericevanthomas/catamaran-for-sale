Most 15-year-old diesels hide deferred maintenance. These don't. The Yanmars were brought down to the frames: rebuilt alternators, every hose and belt replaced, new motor mounts, serviced heat exchangers, engine rooms repainted so any future drip is obvious at a glance. The saildrives are brand-new SD60 units installed in 2025, and the Brunton Auto Props were sent back to the factory rep for new bearings and service.

The combination is genuinely efficient. Seven knots of boat speed at 1,500 RPM in flat conditions — and because she's designed around a long 50' waterline, she runs very happily on one engine. On an ocean crossing you can alternate engines and still hold six knots cruising. Top speed under power is about nine knots.

When you turn the engines off, the Brunton props feather automatically — put them in gear, they lock, and you pick up roughly another knot of sailing speed compared to fixed props.

- 2× Yanmar 3JH5E 40hp diesels — brought down to the frames by current owners
- 2× brand-new SD60 saildrives (2025)
- 2× Brunton 460mm (18") self-pitching auto propellers — sent back to factory rep, new bearings, fully serviced (2025)
