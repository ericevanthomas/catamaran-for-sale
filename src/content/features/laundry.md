This is our second long Caribbean cruise. Our first one was without a washing machine. Our second is with one. The difference is enormous — laundromat runs used to eat a whole day every week and forced us into marinas or towns we didn't want to be in. Now we anchor wherever we want, for as long as we want.

And we rigged permanent drying lines under the cockpit roof — clothes stay out of the rain, so a passing squall doesn't restart your laundry day.

- Samsung automatic washing machine — full cycle, plumbed in
- Located in the port-hull corridor so it doesn't dominate a cabin
- Runs on 120V shore or inverter power from the 1,280Ah lithium house bank
- Dedicated drying lines mounted under the cockpit roof — dry in any weather
