Everything you need to navigate is new, and everything is integrated. The core is a Raymarine Axiom+ 12" chartplotter with a complete Navionics cartography package — the US east coast, the Caribbean, and down into South America. We also added an Orca Display 2 nav display mounted in a self-charging cradle at the nav station but detachable, so you can carry it out to the helm when you want instruments in your hands. It's interfaced with the NMEA2000-connected Orca Core 2 system to tie it into the boat's other electronics and works as either a chartplotter or an instrument display.

We also installed a [Predict Wind Data Hub](https://www.predictwind.com/datahub), which uploads our position via Starlink in real time — friends and family can [follow our boat on our blog](https://sailingtropicalia.com/location/) without us having to manually update our position.

**New electronics (installed by** [**the Boat Doctor**](https://boatdrusa.com/) **in St. Augustine):**

- Raymarine Axiom+ 12" MFD chartplotter — with North America + Caribbean + South America cartography
- Orca Display 2 and Orca Core 2 — Display is removable from the nav station, can move to the helm; interfaced with Raymarine; also shows AIS enriched AIS targets via Starlink (a great safety feature)
- New control pad and computer for the autopilot
- Predict Wind Hub — position tracking via Starlink

**Existing Raymarine integrated system:**

- Raymarine RD418HD Radome (Fall 2023)
- Raymarine ST60 Tridata and Wind Indicator
- Raymarine ST7002 Autopilot with LewMar Rotary Drive
- Raymarine RAY55E VHF Radio with remote speaker in salon
- Raymarine AIS650 Class B Transponder with dedicated antenna
- 2× Raymarine GPS with external antennas
- Handheld Raymarine and Icom VHF radios

**Sonar & other:**

- Garmin dedicated display with Panoptix forward-looking sonar and side-scan sonar (Feb 2023)
- Suunto bulkhead-mount magnetic compass
- Masthead Windex
- Windlass remote and chain counter at the helm
