A brand-new life raft and EPIRB are the headline items, but the upgrade we care about most is a set of AIS-enabled man-overboard beacons built into each of our four life jackets.

Most offshore beacons require the wearer to activate them manually. These don't — the antenna deploys automatically the moment the jacket hits saltwater, and the beacon transmits the swimmer's position back to the boat as an AIS target on the chart plotter. If your kid goes over, they don't have to remember anything. You can steer straight to them.

- New life raft
- New EPIRB in the ditch bag
- AIS-enabled man-overboard beacons on all four life jackets — auto-deploy on water contact, transmit position to the boat as AIS targets
- Personal locator beacons (PLBs)
- New ditch bag stocked for offshore abandonment
- Current, in-date flare kit
- Handheld Raymarine and Icom VHFs in the ditch bag rotation
- Plenty of life jackets and secondary safety gear
